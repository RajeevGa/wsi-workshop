Scope of the Workshop

The Wireless Systems Innovation (WSI) Workshop provides a collaborative forum for advancing experimental research, prototyping, and system design in radio-based communications, sensing, and navigation. WSI brings together researchers, engineers, and practitioners from academia, industry, startups, and open-source communities to share insights and foster innovation.

The two-day program features invited talks from domain experts, technical tutorials, and poster/demo sessions. WSI provides a platform for collaboration, knowledge exchange, and shaping future directions in wireless systems research.

Call for Posters and Demos

WSI Workshop 2025 invites submissions for posters and live demonstrations showcasing innovative research, experimental prototypes, and practical implementations in wireless communications, sensing, and navigation systems. We welcome contributions from researchers, engineers, practitioners, and students working on radio-based systems spanning software-defined radio platforms, next-generation networks, positioning technologies, and emerging applications.

Submission Categories

Posters

Research results, work-in-progress, experimental findings, or system designs suitable for interactive presentation. Recorded demonstrations can be shown along with the poster. Submissions should include an extended abstract (1 page).

Demos

Live demonstrations of prototypes, testbeds, platforms, or applications. Submissions should include a demonstration description (1 page) outlining the system, setup requirements, and key features to be showcased.

All submissions will be peer-reviewed. Accepted posters and demos will be presented in interactive sessions throughout the workshop, providing opportunities for in-depth discussions with attendees.

Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

Software-Defined Radio and Platforms

  • SDR architectures and implementations
  • FPGA-based radio systems
  • Open-source SDR frameworks
  • Real-time signal processing and accelerators

Experimental Testbeds and Emulation

  • Wireless testbed design and deployment
  • Network emulators and simulators
  • Digital-Twins

5G/6G and Next-Generation Networks

  • Open RAN (O-RAN) systems
  • Open-source cellular software (OAI, srsRan)
  • mmWave and MIMO systems

Navigation and Positioning

  • GNSS systems and applications
  • 5G/6G/WiFi-based positioning
  • Indoor localization systems

Spectrum and Sensing

  • Spectrum sensing and monitoring
  • Cognitive radio systems
  • Radar and integrated sensing and communication systems

Emerging Applications

  • IoT and sensor networks
  • Connected robotics and autonomous vehicles
  • Satellite communications
  • Wireless fingerprinting, security and privacy
  • Machine learning for wireless systems
